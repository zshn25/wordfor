About

Why I built this

I'm Zeeshan. While writing — emails, papers, code comments, anything — I constantly run into the same problem: I know the word exists, I can describe exactly what it means, but I just can't recall it. So I'd open Google, type something like "word for fear of being forgotten", scroll through SEO-stuffed listicles, and maybe find it five minutes later (it's athazagoraphobia, by the way).

I got tired of that loop and built WordFor — a reverse dictionary that does one thing well: you describe a concept and it instantly gives you the word. No ads, no sign-ups, no tracking. The AI runs entirely in your browser, so your searches never leave your device.

How it works

1

Describe

Type a description, definition, or feeling in plain English.

2

Match

A sentence-embedding AI model converts your description into a meaning vector and compares it against 110,000+ dictionary definitions using cosine similarity, boosted by keyword overlap.

3

Discover

Get ranked results instantly. Everything runs in your browser — no data ever leaves your device.

Under the hood

Full mode (desktop)

mdbr-leaf-mt — a 22M-parameter sentence encoder running via Transformers.js / ONNX Runtime (WASM or WebGPU), using the optimized ONNX conversion. Uses asymmetric retrieval: definitions encoded at build time by the larger teacher model mxbai-embed-large-v1.

Lite mode (mobile)

potion-base-8M — a Model2Vec static embedding model. Pure JS inference with no runtime dependencies, sub-1ms query speed, and lower memory usage.

Dictionary

Open English WordNet 2025+ — 120,000+ English definitions including proper nouns from Open English Namenet, pre-embedded offline and served as static binary files.

Scoring

Full mode: pure cosine similarity on bare definition embeddings. Lite mode: 70% cosine similarity + 30% keyword overlap. On desktop, lite results appear instantly while the full AI model loads in the background.

API access

WordFor runs entirely in the browser, so there's no traditional REST API. However, you can link directly to searches and embed results:

URL query

Link to a pre-filled search: ?q=fear+of+heights. Add &mode=lite or &mode=full to force a mode.

Embed via iframe

Embed WordFor in your page:
<iframe src="https://wordfor.xyz/?q=your+query" width="100%" height="600"></iframe>

Privacy

WordFor has no server, no database, no analytics, and no cookies. The AI model and dictionary data are downloaded once and cached in your browser. Every search happens locally on your device. Your queries are never sent anywhere.
